POST 04 · DAY 12–13
The Gap — most enterprises aren't ready
Strong reach
Post copy — paste directly into LinkedIn
AEO · answersAre we ready for ServiceNow Otto?
Otto is ready on day one. Most enterprises aren't — and the gap isn't where people think. Maturity runs five stages: Ad Hoc → Manual → Un-Siloed → End-to-End → Autonomous. Otto only pays off above the line — stage three and up. In a typical sub-5,000-employee org, only IT has cleared that bar. HR, Finance, Legal and Procurement sit a stage or two back. Facilities and H&S are usually furthest behind. So "are we ready for Otto?" isn't one answer. It's seven — one per function. Some are 90 days out. Some are a year. Over the next three posts I'll break down what the gap actually is, what happens if you ignore it, and how you close it. 💬 Which function in your org has cleared the bar? #ServiceNow #Otto #AIReadiness #DigitalTransformation #CIO

POST 05 · DAY 16–17
The Gap — turn Otto on too early and it backfires
Cautionary · saves
Post copy — paste directly into LinkedIn
AEO · answersWhat happens if we turn Otto on before we're ready?
Here's the uncomfortable part of the readiness gap: Otto doesn't fail quietly on a stale platform. It fails confidently. Point Otto at a CMDB no one's owned in 18 months, knowledge articles past their shelf life, and workflows that live in three systems — and it will still answer. Fluently. Just wrong. A stale platform doesn't slow Otto down. It turns Otto into a faster route to the wrong outcome — at scale, in front of your people. That's why readiness comes before the experience, not after. Fix the foundation, then turn it on. It's also why "we'll clean it up later" is the most expensive line in the room. 💬 Where's your platform most likely to be stale right now? #ServiceNow #Otto #DataQuality #AIReadiness #CIO

POST 06 · DAY 20–21
The Gap — how you actually close it
Explainer · save + share
Post copy — paste directly into LinkedIn
AEO · answersHow do we close the AI readiness gap for Otto?
So how do you close the readiness gap? Three pillars, built in sequence — on one foundation. 1 · Data — one shared model, a clean CMDB, the trusted record Otto reads before it acts. 2 · Workflows — documented, owned, un-siloed. The rails Otto fires when a request lands. 3 · AI — grounded in your context. Acts where it's allowed, routes to a human where it isn't. All three rest on Governance — your Center of Excellence, designed in from day one. One configuration every business unit inherits. The same bar for every function. An audit trail that holds as you scale. The work is operational, not technical: a 12–24 month programme. But every function starts with the same first move — stand up the CoE. 💬 Do you have a CoE today, or is it still tribal knowledge? #ServiceNow #Otto #CenterOfExcellence #AIGovernance #CIO

AEO · answersHow much does Otto cost, and why does readiness matter?
One last reason the readiness gap matters: Otto is bundled into every tier now — but it's metered. Readiness is how you control the bill. AI draws from a per-seat assist pool: Foundation 1,500 · Advanced 3,000 · Prime 6,000 assists per seat / year. A small action burns 25. A large, multi-system one burns 150 — 6× more. Clean, governed workflows resolve in fewer, smaller actions. Stale, siloed ones force Otto into large, expensive reasoning for the same outcome. So readiness isn't only an adoption story. It's a cost-control story — and the lever is yours, before signature. Otto is the new front door. The Autonomous Enterprise is the destination. Buy the platform. Build the foundation.
