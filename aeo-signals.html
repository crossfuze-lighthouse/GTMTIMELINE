What the data tells us
The pattern in the questions

Buyers aren’t asking whether to adopt ServiceNow — that’s largely decided. The problem repeated across the data is readiness: “we’ve committed to AI, but are we ready to use it, and how do we get there?” It surfaces in IT (CMDB and data foundations) and in every business function (HR, customer and supplier data). Readiness is the key focus for content across both tracks.

Track AAutonomous IT 43 Qs · 3,743 demand
What the data suggests

In IT, readiness means the data foundation. Buyers ask whether CMDB, CSDM and data quality are good enough before switching on autonomous agents — plus how to govern those agents, and how ServiceNow stacks up against Jira and BMC. High-intent and bottom-of-funnel.

What to produce
  • CMDB / CSDM readiness assessments
  • “Fix your data before AI” guides
  • AI-agent governance frameworks
  • Otto vs Moveworks / Einstein / Copilot comparisons
  • ITSM-base defence vs Jira & BMC

What the data suggests

The larger, less-contested track — and the same readiness question repeats in every function: is our HR, customer or supplier data mature enough to automate? Buyers also weigh ServiceNow against incumbents (Workday, Salesforce, Coupa, Ironclad).

What to produce
  • Readiness-by-function: “is your HR / customer / supplier data ready?”
  • “Vs incumbent” comparisons — Workday, Salesforce, Coupa, Ironclad
  • “Orchestrate around, don’t replace” integration stories
  • Sequence: HR & CSM first, then Finance & Legal

What the data says

All 360 questions are real — sourced from ChatGPT, Perplexity and Gemini, demand-ranked, 2+ engines = confirmed. The throughline: whatever buyers are buying, the question underneath is "are we ready?" — and it shows up platform-wide, in IT, and across every business function. Track B (Business Re-Invention) carries the majority of dual-track demand and is the least-contested ground.

What "demand" means: each of ChatGPT, Perplexity and Gemini scored every question 0–100 for how often buyers ask it. Each bar sums those scores across the questions in that track or stage — a longer bar means more questions, asked more often. A question is "confirmed" when 2 or more engines independently raise it.
